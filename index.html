Ph.D. Student in
Social Complexity Science

I am a Ph.D. student at the Research Center for Social Complexity (CICS) at Universidad del Desarrollo. My research focuses on social contagion and diffusion processes within networks, using agent-based models and statistical analysis. With a background in Physics from Universidad de Concepción, I am passionate about applying quantitative methods to understand complex social phenomena.

My most recent works include "Dipole-dipole interactions through a lens" published in Physical Review A, and the ongoing development of the R package "netdiffuseR" for analyzing diffusion processes on networks.
